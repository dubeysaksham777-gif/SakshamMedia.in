Yoga for Healthy Ageing: India Unveils Theme for International Yoga Day 2026

By Saksham Dubey

New Delhi | June 06, 2026

Pic Courtesy: TheSamikhysa

New Delhi, June 6: The Ministry of AYUSH has announced “Yoga for Healthy Ageing” as the official theme for the 12th International Day of Yoga (IDY) 2026, to be observed on June 21. The theme aims to highlight yoga's role in helping people lead longer, healthier, and more active lives as populations around the world continue to age.

The announcement was made by Union Minister of State (Independent Charge) for AYUSH, Prataprao Jadhav, during the Yoga Mahotsav 2026 held at the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Khajuraho. The minister also confirmed that the main national celebration of International Day of Yoga 2026 will take place in Kolkata on June 21.

The theme comes at a time when countries worldwide are facing rapidly ageing populations and a growing burden of lifestyle-related diseases. According to the Ministry of AYUSH, the focus is not only on increasing life expectancy but also on improving “healthspan” — the number of years a person remains healthy, active, and independent

Officials say regular yoga practice can help maintain flexibility, balance, mobility, mental well-being, and emotional resilience, all of which become increasingly important with age. Through a combination of physical postures, breathing exercises, meditation, and mindfulness, yoga offers a holistic approach to healthy ageing and preventive healthcare.

The Ministry is also promoting its “Yoga 365” campaign, encouraging citizens to make yoga a year-round habit rather than a once-a-year activity. More than two lakh people have already participated in the initiative's free yoga training programmes, according to government data

International Day of Yoga was established by the United Nations General Assembly in 2014 following a proposal by Narendra Modi, and the first global observance was held on June 21, 2015. Since then, it has grown into one of the world's largest public health and wellness movements, celebrated in more than 180 countries.
