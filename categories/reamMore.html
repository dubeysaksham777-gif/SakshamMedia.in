New Delhi, June 10: Prime Minister Narendra Modi is on track to surpass Jawaharlal Nehru's record as India's second-longest-serving Prime Minister in consecutive terms, marking a significant milestone in the country's political history.

Having first assumed office on May 26, 2014, Modi has now completed more than 12 years in power and is expected to overtake Nehru's uninterrupted tenure later this year. Nehru served as Prime Minister from August 15, 1947, until his death on May 27, 1964, holding office continuously for nearly 17 years.

The development highlights Modi's enduring political influence and the Bharatiya Janata Party's (BJP) dominance in national politics over the past decade. Under his leadership, the BJP secured three consecutive victories in the 2014, 2019, and 2024 Lok Sabha elections.

While Nehru remains India's longest-serving Prime Minister in consecutive terms, Modi's upcoming milestone is being viewed as another landmark achievement in modern Indian politics. Political observers say the record reflects both his personal popularity and the BJP's strong organizational presence across the country.

Modi continues his third term, attention is likely to remain focused on his government's policy agenda and its impact on India's political landscape in the years ahead.
